Irina Pelman is a professional cellist and teacher living and teaching in San Francisco. She represents a well-known Russian cello and piano school.

Irina offers individual and group cello and piano lessons in her studio and via Skype for all ages and ability levels. Her education and broad experience, spanning over 30 years, make her teaching style very interesting and engaging, helping to motivate her students from 4 to 60 years old achieve rapid results.

Irina's primary goal is to help students not only to study, read, understand and play well, but to continue to love and appreciate music. She helps students to achieve all their musical goals.

Irina Pelman has been living and teaching in her big beautiful music studio in San Francisco since 2004.

She began playing cello at age 6.
She received a bachelors degree from the
Stolyarsky Special Music School in her native city of Odessa, Ukraine. She then went on to receive her master's degree under professor G. Pekker at the Novosibirsk Conservatory of Music.

Irina performed as a soloist with symphony orchestras in Russia, particularly such beautiful and technically difficult pieces as the Dvorak Concerto, Rococo Variations by Tchaikovsky and the Concerto-Symphony by Prokofiev.
She also performed widely in festivals and chamber music competitions in Russia.

At the age of 20 she began her teaching career in music schools and college, continuing the reknowned tradition of Russian music pedagogy. She has taught in Novosibirsk (Russia), Odessa, Tel-Aviv, always with excellent professional results. Her students have gone on to receive bachelors and masters degrees in the conservatories of Moscow and Novosibirsk.
